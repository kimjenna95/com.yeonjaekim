Pollie Pollie

An interactive mobile app to create your own polls and debate trending topics with friends and the community.

Project Brief

Pollie Pollie derives from the word Poll. It is a mobile app to allow users to create polls and get real-time feedback from the general public. It collects usable insights, answers, and feedback through polls anyone can create.

Challenge

Pollie Pollie was an idea without any visualization. The challenge of this project was to build a poll app that was not only educational, but a social platform to connect with the community. The goal was to help the user empathize with the public and have a much more in-depth social interaction.

Competitive Market
Research

What apps are being used for people to create their own polls?

Based on user interviews, I researched several apps and products that were commonly used when creating a poll.

Personas

Going further through the research, I identified 2 personas that have different needs and goals. This data will be useful to improve the user experience for each one of them.

Style Guides

As a poll app should be informative, I aimed the style to be modern and reliable.

Usability Testing

I collected and measured user feedbacks to evaluate the prototype. I allowed the users to navigate the app freely without any help - just like how anyone would download and use the app from the App Store. This helped reflect the suitability for its context of use, usability problem predictions, and design suggestions.

Conclusion

There had to be multiple iterations and usability testing to figure out if the app is user-friendly and meets users’ needs. The project was a go-back-and-forth process where I had to take out or integrate a design feature. In the end, we were able to settle down to a design that worked and made sense.

What I Learned

After having worked as a team with a developer, I had a much better understanding of how developers think. Throughout the interactive process, I learned that understanding the developer’s thought process and needs are just as important in our profession as being an outstanding designer.  Not only was I creating the design — I also had to oversee the implementation of the mockups by the developer. My first experience of me trying to collaborate with a developer was hard. However, it made me realize that if I wanted to be a good UX designer, I needed to understand the technical part of creating a digital product.
